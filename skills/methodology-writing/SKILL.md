# Methodology Writing

## Overview

Use this skill to write methods prose that explains choices rather than merely listing steps. Make the section readable, defensible, and specific to the study design.

## Core Workflow

### 1. Establish the design logic first

Before drafting, identify:

- the research design
- the unit of analysis
- the source of data
- the reason this design fits the research question

Do not open with procedure details before the reader knows the logic of the design.

### 2. Move from design to execution

Structure the section in this order unless the journal format requires otherwise:

1. research design and rationale
2. sample, case, or data source
3. measures, coding, or operationalization
4. analytic procedure
5. validity, reliability, robustness, or credibility checks

### 3. Explain choices, not just actions

When describing a method, answer both:

- what was done
- why that choice was appropriate here

Avoid thin template sentences that only say a technique was used.

## Style Controls

- Prefer concrete nouns over broad labels such as "methodological approach" when a more exact term is available.
- Keep terminology consistent once a design choice has been named.
- Avoid inflated certainty. If a method reduces a threat, do not write as though it eliminates it.
- Use [citation-discipline.md](../../shared/references/citation-discipline.md) when the section includes method citations.
- Use [anti-ai-prose-checklist.md](../../shared/references/anti-ai-prose-checklist.md) before finalizing.

## Final Check

Before returning a draft, confirm that the reader can answer:

- what design was used
- why it fit the question
- how the data were produced
- how the constructs were represented
- how quality and limitations were handled
