# Theory Dialogue Writing

## Overview

Use this skill to make academic prose speak to theory directly. The goal is not merely to restate empirical findings, but to clarify what those findings refine, differentiate, constrain, or extend in the underlying theory.

## Core Workflow

### 1. Identify the theoretical object of the paragraph

Before drafting, specify:

- the theory, construct, or mechanism under discussion
- the empirical pattern that matters for it
- the precise conceptual move the paragraph should make

Possible conceptual moves include:

- refinement
- boundary specification
- mechanism clarification
- comparison between competing explanations
- conceptual differentiation

### 2. Write from empirical pattern to theoretical consequence

A strong paragraph usually moves in this order:

1. name the empirical relationship or distinction
2. clarify its direction or condition
3. state what this means for the theory
4. explain how the theory should be narrowed, extended, or differentiated

Do not stop at "this finding contributes to theory" without saying how.

### 3. Keep theory language analytic

- Use theory terms to make distinctions, not to decorate the paragraph.
- If several theories are cited, specify what each one explains differently.
- When evidence is mixed, explain what kind of theoretical uncertainty remains.

## Output Preference

When the user asks for a discussion or theory paragraph, first identify the empirical pattern, the direction or condition that matters, and the conceptual move the paragraph should make. Return theory-facing prose by default unless the user explicitly asks for a rubric-based diagnosis or a separate conceptual outline.

## Final Check

Before returning a draft, confirm that the paragraph tells the reader:

- what empirical pattern is under discussion
- what direction or condition matters
- what theoretical distinction follows from it
- why that distinction is not just a paraphrase of the results
