# Theory Dialogue Rubric

Use this rubric when writing conceptual discussion or theory-facing review prose.

## Minimum Standard

The paragraph must do more than report that a relationship exists.

It should clarify at least one of the following:

- what type of theoretical relationship is at stake
- what direction the evidence supports
- what boundary condition changes the theoretical expectation
- what mechanism is more plausible than competing explanations
- where existing theory is underspecified, too broad, or internally inconsistent

## Warning Signs

- the paragraph is a prose version of the results section
- theory terms are named but not used analytically
- the conclusion remains "X matters for Y" without clarifying how
- citations are present, but no conceptual distinction is drawn

## Stronger Move

After naming the empirical pattern, explain what it means for the theory.

Ask:

- Does this evidence refine the theory?
- Does it narrow the conditions under which the theory applies?
- Does it show a mechanism, sequence, or comparison the theory previously blurred?
